## General Positioning

### Q: How is GetAnswers different from other email tools?

**A:** Most email tools fall into three categories:

1. **Email Clients** (Superhuman, Hey.com, Canary) - Make you faster at *writing* emails
2. **Organization Tools** (SaneBox, Boomerang) - Filter and organize your inbox
3. **AI Assistants** (Ellie, Compose AI) - Suggest replies or autocomplete

**GetAnswers is an AI Email Agent** - It actually writes your complete email responses. You just approve, edit, or escalate.

The key difference: **Other tools help you manage email. GetAnswers does it for you.**

---

### Q: Is this just autocomplete or smart replies?

**A:** No. GetAnswers generates **complete, contextual email responses** that are ready to send.

- **Not autocomplete** (like Compose AI) - Full responses, not sentence fragments

You review the draft in an approval queue and click approve, edit, or escalate. That's it.

## Trust & Control

### Q: What if the AI writes something wrong or offensive?

**A:** Multiple safety layers:

**Layer 1: Approval Queue** (default)
- Every AI response goes to approval queue
- You review before sending
- Click approve, edit, or reject

**Layer 2: Confidence Scoring**
- AI rates its own confidence (0-100%)
- Low confidence ‚Üí Always flagged for review
- High confidence ‚Üí Can be auto-sent (if configured)

**Layer 3: Risk Assessment**
- Automatic categorization: Low / Medium / High risk
- High-risk emails always require manual review
- Examples: Legal, financial, first-time senders

**Layer 4: Learning from Edits**
- When you edit AI responses, it learns
- Patterns improve over time
- Your writing style adapts

**Bottom line**: You're always in control. AI assists, you approve.

---

### Q: Can I trust the AI to handle important emails?

**A:** Start small, build trust:

**Week 1-2: Review everything**
- Let AI draft all responses
- Review and edit in approval queue
- Get comfortable with AI quality

**Week 3-4: Selective auto-handling**
- Configure low-risk senders for auto-send
- Example: Newsletters, confirmations, routine updates
- Still review medium/high-risk

**Week 5+: Autonomous handling**
- 80%+ of emails auto-handled
- Only review flagged items
- Focus on strategic emails

**Trust but verify**: You can always review the approval queue history to see what was sent.
